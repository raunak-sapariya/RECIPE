Champagne & Luxardo Cherry Cake



Give your halloween party the scariest centerpiece with this Bloody Red Velvet Cake. Decadent sponges covered in classic cream cheese frosting make this cake too tempting to be afraid of.
Prep Time: 25 mins
Cook Time: 25-30 mins
Total Time: 1hr
Servings: 6 people
Equipments: stand or hand mixer and 3 x 6in cake tins



Ingredients:
  • 310g self raising Flour
  • 8g cocoa powder
  • 115g butter
  • 200g sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 200g sunflower oil
  • 3 tablespoons milk or Buttermilk
  • 11g vanilla extract
  • 5g red food colouring
  • 1 teaspoon bicarbonate soda


  • 115g unsalted Butter
  • 200g cream Cheese
  • 1 vanilla extract optional
  • 375g icing sugar
  • ¬º salt optional


Directions:
Sponge
  1. Preheat your oven to 180C
  2. Mix together the flour, cocoa and bicarbonate soda into a bowl then put aside
  3. Combine butter and sugar in a new clean mixing bowl
  4. Add eggs one at a time. Mixing until fully incorporated each time.
  5. Mix in the oil, buttermilk, vanilla extract
  6. Grab your dry ingredients and sieve into the bowl then mix
  7. Add to cake tins and bake for 25-30 mins
  8. Check with a skewer to see if cakes are ready; if it comes out gooey it needs more time in the oven.
  9. Place the butter in the mixer
  10. Add the cream cheese and combine till mixture is incorporated (~3 minutes).
  11. Gradually add icing sugar using your spatula mix it in.
  12. Once you‚Äôve added all your icing sugar, turn your electric mixer to medium speed and mix until the cream cheese icing thickens.
  13. Add red food colouring to your Icing to make it nice and bloody!
Assembly:
  1. Grab your cake board place it on your turntable
  2. Put a spoon full of buttercream on the bottom on your board to stop your cake from sliding so you're ready to start stacking
  3. Place one sponge in the middle board and add in your bloody cream cheese filling then stack the second layer. Repeat for the third layer.
  4. Now coat the cake in your cream cheese icing and place it in the fridge to firm up.
  5. Add knife slices around your cake to give it a scary bloody effect or melt down some candy melts to make dangerous shards.
Tips:
  1. Don't overmix your batter or your cake will be dry and tough.
  2. Make sure your butter and cream cheese are completely smooth before adding in the icing sugar. No one wants a lumpy icing!
  3. For bold decorations, get some candy melts and make them into shards for a bit of extra danger.
